The **Calendar** lets you declare your absences and lateness for this guild, either as a one-off or on a recurring basis — handy for letting your officers know your availability before a raid gets scheduled.

![Calendar overview](/assets/manual/en/guild/calendar-overview.png)

### Declaring a one-off absence or lateness

Click **Declare an absence/lateness**, or directly on a day in the calendar. Choose a single date or a date range, a status (**Absent**, **Partial**, or **Available** to override a recurrence on that specific day), and an optional reason.

The **Partial** status adds two optional time bounds — *available from* and *available until*. Use either one, or both: lateness is declared with only the start time, an early departure with only the end time.

A one-off declaration always takes precedence over an active recurring pattern on the same day.

### Adding a recurring pattern

Click **Add a recurring pattern** for something that repeats regularly: an evening blocked out every week, or a shift rotation (e.g. a 5-days-on, 2-days-off cycle). Pick **Weekly** mode to check weekdays directly, or **Advanced** mode to define a cycle of any length with its own start date.

Each day of the cycle is set to **Available**, **Absent**, or **Partial**, with the same optional reason and time bounds as a one-off declaration.

### Editing or stopping a recurring pattern

Editing or stopping a recurring pattern is **never retroactive**: days that have already passed keep exactly the status they had at the time, even if you change the rule afterward. The change only applies from today onward.

### Editing or deleting a one-off declaration

The **One-off** and **Recurring** panels on the right list all your active declarations. A declaration that has already fully elapsed can no longer be edited or deleted — it's the historical record of what was actually declared at the time, and it stays intact.
